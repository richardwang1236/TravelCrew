## 2. 🎒 Pre-Trip Preparation

### Packing Checklist
- **Documents:** Passport (if international), printed hotel & flight confirmations, travel insurance card
- **Clothing:** Light layers (T-shirts, long-sleeve shirt, light jacket for cloudy/evening), comfortable walking shoes, rain jacket (just in case)
- **Accessories:** Sunglasses, hat, reusable water bottle, small backpack for day trips
- **Tech:** Phone charger/power bank, camera, earbuds for transit
- **Health:** Basic first-aid kit, any personal medication, hand sanitizer

### Local Policies & Precautions
- **Visa:** Most international visitors need an eTA (Electronic Travel Authorization) or visa — check Canada's immigration website before travel
- **Reservation Rules:** Book CN Tower tickets online in advance to skip lines
- **Tipping:** Standard 15–20% at restaurants; 10–15% for taxi/ride-share
- **Smoking:** Prohibited in all indoor public spaces and within 9 meters of building entrances

### Best Travel Tips
- **Best Season:** July is peak summer — warm but not scorching, with long daylight hours
- **Off-Peak Times:** Visit major attractions early (9:00 AM) to avoid midday crowds
- **Rainy-Day Alternatives:** Ripley's Aquarium and Casa Loma are excellent indoor options
- **Photo Spots:** CN Tower observation deck at golden hour, HTO Park beach, Nathan Phillips Square with the iconic "TORONTO" sign

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Toronto's TTC (subway, streetcar, bus) is efficient. Get a Presto card at any subway station for easy tap-and-go access.

## 7. 🛡️ Safety & Avoidance Guide

### 🚫 Tourist Traps to Avoid
| Trap | Why to Avoid |
|------|-------------|
| **Dundas Square (Day 1)** | Overcrowded with street performers, aggressive panhandlers, and overpriced chain stores |
| **Kensington Market on Weekends (Day 2)** | Extremely crowded; visit on a weekday morning for a more authentic experience |
| **Ripley's Aquarium Gift Shop** | Overpriced souvenirs — buy elsewhere |
| **CN Tower Photo Packages** | Expensive ($30+ CAD) — take your own photos with a phone or camera |

### ⚠️ Common Scams
- **"Charity" Donations:** People with clipboards asking for donations to fake charities — politely say no and walk away
- **Taxi Overcharging:** Use ride-sharing apps or official taxi stands at the airport and major hotels
- **Street "Games":** Three-card monte or shell games near Dundas Square — these are rigged
